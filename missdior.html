Miss Dior Exhibition Rebranding

Type

Personal Project
Duration

4 Weeks
Jul - Aug 2020
Role

Research, Identity Design,
Custom Type

Overview

Miss Dior, as one of the most beloved perfume lines from Dior, is well known for soft, lovely scents highlighting femininity. Miss Dior Exhibition was more than a new product showcase: It was a meaningful event to look back history of Miss Dior and remember the founder and designer of the brand, Christian Dior.

However, the original poster uses rough strokes for its design, and the title of exhibition, Love N’Roses, only focuses on promoting the new product, Rose N’Roses. As a result, the elegance of Miss Dior and the meaning of the exhibition is lacking.

Design Challenge

Redesign visual identity of Miss Dior Exhibition to better convey the purpose of the exhibition and represent Miss Dior.

Solution

Concept

The new exhibition concept Design for Women is inspired by the perspective of Christian Dior that perfume is more than a pleasing scent for a woman. It also aligns with his passion to imbue Miss Dior perfumes with gracefulness and elegance.

“A woman’s perfume tells more about her than her handwriting.”

Colour

The chosen colours still stay true to Miss Dior’s brand identity. Muted purple is associated with Miss Dior’s brand colour, and it illustrates the value of grace with the support of black background.

Type

Custom ribbon typeface with distinctive lettering is used primarily for poster titles. The movement and colour of ribbons represents sophistication and femininity.

To have a clean look, Josefin Sans is used for everything else other than poster titles. It is geometric and elegant typeface which matches well with the ribbon type.

Customer Interaction

The end part of each ticket is a perfume blotter which contains one of the Miss Dior scents. Ticket holders can scan the QR code at the back, send in their answer, and receive limited edition gifts.

Takeaways

Design from other’s perspective.

In order to illustrate that this exhibition itself is an exploration of all aspects surrounding Miss Dior, I had to think like Christian Dior. To see what he wanted to realize through this creation, I tried to read the thoughts and beliefs behind of his design. This experience was a valuable practice of designing from others’ perspective.

Consistency is the key.

All promotional materials can vary within a common design language, so field of exhibition itself requires a strong visual identity. It is crucial to keep a consistent identity throughout every included object.
