Overview

Miss Dior, one of the most beloved perfume lines from Dior, is well known for its soft, lovely scents. The Miss Dior Exhibition was more than a new perfume showcase, as it looked back at the history of Miss Dior and remembered the founder, Christian Dior.

The original poster uses rough strokes for its design which does not match with the elegance of Miss Dior. In addition, the title of exhibition, Love N’Roses, only focuses on promoting the new product, Rose N’Roses, and thus undermines the larger purpose of the exhibition.

Design Challenge

Redesign visual identity of Miss Dior Exhibition to better convey the purpose of the exhibition and more truly represent Miss Dior.

Solution

Concept

The new exhibition concept Design for Women is inspired by the perspective of Christian Dior that perfume is more than a pleasing scent for a woman. It also aligns with his passion to imbue Miss Dior perfumes with gracefulness and elegance.

“A woman’s perfume tells more about her than her handwriting.”

Colour

The chosen colours stay true to Miss Dior’s brand identity. Muted purple is associated with the brand colour, and with the support of black background, it illustrates the value of grace.

Typography

A custom ribbon typeface with distinctive lettering is used primarily for poster titles. The movement and colour of ribbons represents sophistication and femininity.

To have a clean look, Josefin Sans is used for everything else. It is a geometric and elegant typeface which matches well with the ribbon type.

Takeaways

Design from another’s perspective

In order to illustrate that this exhibition itself is an exploration of many aspects surrounding Miss Dior, I decided to think like Christian Dior. To see what he wanted to realize through his creations, I tried to read the thoughts behind his design. This experience led to a valuable practice.

Consistency is the key

All promotional materials can vary within a common design language, so an exhibition itself requires a coherent visual identity for all its parts.
