Pride Month Campaign

Type

Individual Project
Duration

2 Weeks
Aug - Sep 2020
Role

Planning, Public Service
Advertisement, Campaign Design

OVERVIEW

While Canadian society is perceived as LGBTQ+ friendly in general, the community still face prejudice and discrimination. This pride month campaign is hypothetically held by Love Has No Labels during pride month in Toronto, Canada.

Love Has No Labels  is “a movement to promote diversity, equity and inclusion of all people.” By utilizing one of their slogans, love has no sexuality, the purpose of this campaign is to advocate for LGBTQ+ people’s rights.

Design Challenge

Design persuasive posters to deliver the message that LGBTQ+ people are no different from everyone else.

Solution

Concept

The design concept is based on the different colours from the rainbow flag, which contains deeper meanings.

Colour & Texture

The gradation from darker to brighter colours illustrates the hope that biases and pre-conceived notions can be changed, along with the variations of triangular shapes that shows the diversity of love.

The overall bright colour scheme with subtle grain texture is used to easily attract attention.

Type

The sans-serif typeface Avenir is used in its heavy weight to deliver the campaign’s messages very clearly and straightforwardly with the added benefit of familiarity.

Avenir Black

Heading

Avenir, 1/2 of its heading

Subheading

Language

The meaning of each rainbow flag colour is used as main headings on the poster, with slight changes to help better encapsulate the original connotation.

The modes of persuasion: ethos, pathos, and logos were taken into consideration to create convincing copies.

Ethos

Love Has No Label has valid credit as a social movement, and the dashed frame in its logo is utilized in the posters to represent embracement and credibility.

Pathos

Implies the rights that we take for granted are not applicable for everyone.

Everyone has the right to be happy as you do.

Logos

Emphasizes beliefs that everyone would agree with.

Everyone is equal.

Takeaways

Location is an important factor to consider.

Since these posters are meant for public places such as subway stations to reach as many people as possible, they shouldn’t be complicated but clear and straightforward. Variations of triangular shapes and vibrant colours are sufficiently effective in catching people’s eyes in busy places.
