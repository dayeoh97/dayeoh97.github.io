OVERVIEW

While Canadian society is perceived as LGBTQ2+ friendly in general, the community still faces prejudice and discrimination. This pride month campaign is hypothetically held by Love Has No Labels during pride month in Toronto.

Love Has No Labels  is an already-existing organization “to promote diversity, equity and inclusion of all people.” By utilizing their logo and one of their slogans, “love has no sexuality”, the purpose of this campaign is to advocate for LGBTQ2+ people’s rights.

Design Challenge

Design persuasive posters to deliver the message that LGBTQ+ people are no different from everyone else.

Solution

Concept

The design concept is based on the different colours from the rainbow flag, each of which represents deeper meanings.

Colour & Texture

The gradation from darker to brighter colours illustrates the hope that biases and pre-conceived beliefs can be changed. The variations of triangular shapes show the diversity of love.

The overall bright colour scheme with subtle grain texture is used to attract attention easily.

Type

The sans-serif typeface Avenir is used in its heavy weight to deliver the campaign’s messages very clearly and straightforwardly with the added benefit of familiarity.

Language

The meaning of each rainbow flag colour is used as main headings on the poster. Some of the meanings have been altered in order to make the word more explicit and to make each word fit the design.

Takeaways

Location is an important factor to consider.

These posters are meant for public places, such as subway stations, in order to reach as many people as possible. They shouldn’t be complicated but clear and straightforward. Variations of triangular shapes and vibrant colours are sufficiently effective for catching people’s eyes in busy places.
