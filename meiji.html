Overview

Meiji is a large Japanese snack company which exports internationally. This hypothetical project will have Meiji sponsoring the Tokyo 2020 Olympics. The project’s goal is to increase interest in the Japanese language by using simple words on Meiji’s product packaging, and thereby increase its sales.

Design Challenge

Promote the culture and language of the host country of the universally-loved event through collaboration with the local snack company.

Solution

Concept

Three fruits are chosen due to their short and easy-to-pronounce names: ichigo (strawberry), budo (grape), and momo (peach). They will be used in the ice-cream bar and yogurt drink which are Meiji’s two best-selling products.

Colour

Pastel colours on custom typeface highlight Meiji’s catchy and cute package design, and their softness works well with dairy products.

Typography

This appetizing display typeface is associated with soft, milky products like ice-cream bars and yogurt drinks.

Heavy weights at the bottom and teardrop shapes are akin to dripping liquid.

The overall round shapes and high x-height make the typeface look cute and friendly.

Its unique curvy lines create a harmonious fluid flow.

Takeaways

Designing a typeface is more than creating letters.

I’ve realized that designing a typeface is an intricate process of dissecting and analyzing letterforms. This has allowed me to design more coherent and meaningful typography.

Foreign language can be a constructive element for design.

When the context is appropriate to use foreign languages, they are an amusing asset to work with. Researching a new language, and how its letterforms are used extended my design knowledge.
